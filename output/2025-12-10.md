# NORi: ML-Augmented Ocean Boundary Layer Parameterization

**Main Contribution:**
Develops NORi, a hybrid physics-neural network parameterization for ocean boundary layer turbulence that combines Richardson number-dependent diffusivity closures with neural ODEs to capture entrainment dynamics. Achieves superior generalization and numerical stability compared to traditional local diffusive closures.

**Method:**
- Physics base: Richardson number (Ri)-dependent diffusivity/viscosity controls parameterization
- Neural component: Neural ODEs trained to capture non-local entrainment at boundary layer base
- Training approach: A posteriori calibration using time-integrated loss functions on actual variables of interest rather than noisy instantaneous subgrid fluxes
- Realistic seawater equation of state incorporated

**Data:**
Large-eddy simulation (LES) datasets spanning 2-day training horizons; tested across varying convective strengths, stratifications, rotation rates, and wind forcings.

**Climate Relevance:**
Ocean boundary layer parameterization is critical for climate models; accurate entrainment representation directly impacts heat and momentum transport, affecting sea surface temperature predictions and large-scale ocean circulation.

**Implications:**
Enables climate models to use longer time steps (1 hour vs. typical 10-minute requirements) while maintaining stability over century-scale integrations, reducing computational cost without sacrificing accuracy.

**Limitations:**
Training limited to 2-day horizons; generalization to extreme conditions beyond training distribution not explicitly discussed; applicability to other boundary layer regimes (e.g., stable stratification) unclear.

**TL;DR:**
Physics-informed neural networks enable accurate, stable ocean boundary layer parameterizations trainable on short simulations but valid for century-scale climate integrations.
