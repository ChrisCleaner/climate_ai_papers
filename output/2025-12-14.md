# Research Summary: ML Downscaling for Renewable Energy Droughts

**Main Contribution:**
Develops machine learning emulators trained on CMIP5/CORDEX data to efficiently downscale global climate models (CMIP6) to regional resolution, eliminating the computational burden of running multiple regional climate models while maintaining accuracy for renewable energy impact assessments.

**Method:**
Neural network emulators learn the mapping between coarse-resolution global climate fields and fine-resolution regional climate outputs, trained on CMIP5 and CORDEX simulations, then applied to unseen CMIP6 data to test generalization and stability.

**Data:**
CMIP5 global simulations, CORDEX regional climate model ensemble, CMIP6 global projections, and regional observational data for Central Europe; focus on wind speed and solar radiation variables.

**Climate Relevance:**
Addresses the critical gap between global climate projections and regional-scale information needed for climate impact assessments, particularly for renewable energy planning in Europe where wind-solar co-occurrence droughts are climate-sensitive.

**Implications:**
Provides a scalable, computationally efficient alternative to coordinated regional modeling efforts, enabling rapid generation of high-resolution climate projections for sector-specific planning (energy infrastructure, grid stability) across multiple climate scenarios.

**Limitations:**
Generalization tested only on CMIP6; regional scope limited to Central Europe; stability of ML emulators for extreme events or longer time horizons not explicitly addressed; reliance on training data quality and representativeness.

**TL;DR:**
Machine learning emulators efficiently downscale global climate models to regional resolution, revealing decreasing future energy droughts in Central Europe while reducing computational costs compared to traditional regional modeling.

# Research Summary: Remote Soil Nutrient Analysis via AI

**Main Contribution:**

Combines physics-informed covariates from radiative transfer models with deep learning embeddings for robust, spatially-validated predictions.

**Method:**
- Hybrid approach: indirect modeling (soil proxies/drivers) + direct spectral modeling
- Physics-informed covariates from radiative transfer models (RTMs)
- Foundation model embeddings for nonlinear feature extraction
- Rigorous validation: spatial blocking, stratified splits, independent test sets to prevent spatial overfitting

**Data:**
Harmonized dataset covering European cropland soils across diverse pedoclimatic zones; evaluation includes spatial cross-validation and independent test set.

**Climate Relevance:**
Soil organic carbon and nitrogen are critical carbon cycle indicators; scalable soil monitoring supports climate mitigation strategies and carbon accounting for agricultural emissions reduction.

**Implications:**
Enables accessible, automated soil assessment for agricultural decision-making and carbon markets; framework is extensible to other regions and soil-dependent applications requiring quantitative environmental monitoring.

**Limitations:**
